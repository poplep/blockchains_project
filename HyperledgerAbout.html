Hyperledger


Hyperledger is an architecture that revolves around the focus that business needs of blockchains can vary.
Hyperledger plans to give a standard platform that can allow modular extensions in order to fit to a users desires.
This means that a user of hyperledger can adjust to allow a focus on faster consenions or more scalability based on what the blockchain will be used for.
Hyperledger's main focuses are on a modular system with high security that is natively not based on cryptocurrency.
Hyperledger consensus processing is based on an environment of partial trust, much different than bitcoin or ether who rely on anonymous nodes.
This is because hyperledger is based on a business sense where nodes will most likely be trusted as they will be within a closed secure system.
Consensus can be approached in multiple ways, but hyperledger focuses on three, permissioned lottery-based, permissioned voting-based, and stand proof of work.
However, based on the modularity of hyperledger any other methods of consensus can possibly be implemented.
Hyperledger essentiality gives a platform that provides interface between modular aspects so they may be modified without affecting how other pieces work.
Hyperledger fabric is just one way to modify hyper ledgers architure in a unique way so it may perform differently, there is also Hyperledger Sawtooth, Burrow, Iroha and Indy.
All of which have unique features that allow them to function different from one another but are all based on the same architecture.
